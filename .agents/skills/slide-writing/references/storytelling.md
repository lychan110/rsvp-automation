\# Storytelling and Tone Rules



Read this file when drafting or rewriting slide narrative.



\## Storytelling Arc



1\. Audience is the hero; presenter/content is the guide.

2\. Use a clear arc:

&#x20;  - Background and motivation

&#x20;  - Why it matters to team/stakeholders/company

&#x20;  - Method

&#x20;  - Evidence/results

&#x20;  - Conclusion tied back to benefits

&#x20;  - Future outlook

3\. Use contrast: what is vs what could be.

4\. Include one memorable proof point (concrete comparison or benchmark).

5\. Prefer bullets over dense paragraphs.

6\. Use quote blocks for high-impact statements.



\## Default Slide Flow



Follow this order unless the user specifies otherwise:



1\. Background

2\. Motivation

3\. Method overview

4\. Core capabilities

5\. Why this approach

6\. Forward path

7\. Conclusions

8\. Backup slides



\## Writing and Tone Rules



1\. Use end-user language, not developer language.

2\. Frame around capabilities (what it enables), not internal mechanics.

3\. No dated timeline framing unless explicitly requested.

4\. Show rationale and tradeoffs.

5\. State risks and prerequisites clearly.

6\. Use flowing narrative language; avoid brusque or cold phrasing.

7\. If uncommon terms are used, define them immediately in plain language.

8\. Avoid jargon-heavy labels in slide copy such as "determinism", "jurisdiction", "gates", "typed", "technical mechanics", and "boundaries".
